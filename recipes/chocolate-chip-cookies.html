Absolute Best High Altitude Chocolate Chip Cookies

These cookies bake up well in Flagstaff, Arizona, about 7000 feet or 2134 meters elevation. They work in the big oven and also in a small toaster oven. See Variations and Reccomendations for details on making them just right.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt

  • 1 cup butter aka two sticks (softened, not melted)
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp water

  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (optional)

Steps

  1. Let any refrigerated ingredients come up to room temp.
  2. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C)
  3. Combine flour, soda, salt in a small bowl.
  4. Cream together butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, vanilla in a large bowl. Mix for at least five minutes. Watch for the color change and then mix for another minute or two.
  5. Add eggs, one at a time, to the butter mixture, beating well after each addition.
  6. Gradually beat in flour mixture and water into the wet ingredients until well combined.
  7. Stir in chips and nuts. One might even fold them in.
  8. Drop by rounded tablespoon onto ungreased baking sheets.
  9. Bake 8-10 minutes or until golden brown.
  10. Cool on baking sheets 2 minutes, then transfer to wire racks.

Variations and Recommendations

  • Bake on parchement paper for best results. This seems to work way better than silpats; I am really unsure of why.
  • I don't actually measure when I put the dough on the baking sheet. I just put a hunk of dough that looks about right. Experiment to find what you like best.
  • If you can stand to wait, refrigerate 30 minutes or more before baking.
  • To have fresh cookies at any time with almost no work:
    1. Prepare freezer paper or a silpat, on a freezer safe sturdy surface that will fit in the freezer, like a plastic plate or wooden cutting board.
    2. Spoon out dough as if you were going to bake it, but onto the freezer paper.
    3. Freeze.
    4. Put frozen dough balls in a freezer bag or freezer safe container. Pyrex food storange container lids don't survive the freezer for me, so I use a freezer bag or cheap takeout plastic containers.
    5. Repeat until all dough is frozen.
    6. Bake from frozen, a minute or two longer than baking from unfrozen.
    7. Date your container. Search for more info if you are concerned, but I would toss them after a month. Like they would last a month in my house, lol.
  • Make sure your baking soda isn't expired.
  • I put a lot of vanilla because vanilla is delicious; reduce the vanilla to 1 tsp and increase water to 1 tbsp if that is more to your taste.
  • To reduce sugar a bit, leave out the white sugar. The cookies are more cakey but not by much, and are still great.
  • If you want more chocolate, increase the chocolate chips to 2 cups, or whatever works for you.
  • I almost never cool on wire racks. I bet they would be better if I did but I have no patience.
  • For a pan cookie (10×15 inch jelly-roll pan), prepare dough as above, and spread into greased pan, baking 17-19 minutes. (I've never done this, but it was in the original recipe)
